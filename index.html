Why are Derivatives Key to Malaysia's Rice Security?

Paddy MR219 is the backbone of Malaysian rice cultivation, praised for its high potential yield. To optimize fertilizer dosage and timing, crop scientists do not just look at plant height \(f(t)\). Instead, they must monitor the instantaneous growth rate, which is the mathematical first derivative \(f'(t)\).

The moment when growth speed reaches its peak (the inflection point of the sigmoid curve) represents the maximum vegetative nutrient uptake window. Applying fertilizer (specifically Urea for Nitrogen enrichment) precisely right before this peak ensures maximum conversion efficiency, prevents environmental runoff, and ensures healthy panicle development.

Biological Model Formulation

Paddy Height Model Function:
$$f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$
Growth Speed Derivative \(f'(t)\):
$$f'(t) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t - t_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)})^2}$$

• \(L\) = Max potential mature height (e.g., 105 cm)

• \(k\) = Growth acceleration rate

• \(t_0\) = Middle transition day (inflection point)

• \(t\) = Days After Transplanting (DAT)
